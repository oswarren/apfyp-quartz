Forty consecutive photographed pieces of bare, unglazed dark stoneware — the largest reviewed stretch of the series so far. Every piece's price is its number times one cent, so this range runs from \$17.27 to \$17.69. Three numbers inside the span (1732, 1736, 1738) have no photographed listing and no page; a missing number isn't gone, it just hasn't had its picture taken.

The whole run is [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] — pinched and coiled, with torn, uneven rims — and left as [[bare-clay|bare clay]] rather than glazed. Within that one family, the photos show several distinct textures:

- **Pale speckling in the clay** — [[embedded-particles|embedded particles]] the store calls "groggy": [[1759]], [[1760]], [[1761]] (the heaviest), [[1762]]
- **Dark protruding grit** — [[surface-nodules|surface nodules]]: [[1729]], [[1730]], [[1733]], [[1735]], [[1737]], [[1739]], [[1741]], [[1742]], [[1744]], [[1745]], [[1746]]
- **Pale wash-like patches** — a thin light [[pale-wash|coating]] over the dark body: [[1748]] (with its number incised in the base), [[1763]], [[1764]], [[1768]], [[1769]]
- **Blue accents** — tiny glassy [[blue-flecks|blue flecks]]: [[1750]], [[1751]]
- **Cloth left in the clay** — [[fabric-impression|fabric impressions]]: [[1757]], [[1758]]
- **Cut-in texture** — allover [[scored-marks|scored marks]]: [[1756]]
- **Pieces that photograph brown** — the clay body is black (the maker confirms it), but in these listing photos the fired surfaces read warm brown, and the piece pages say so: [[1740]]–[[1747]]
