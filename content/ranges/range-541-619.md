Sixty-eight photographed pieces running \$5.41 to \$6.19 — the longest reviewed stretch of the series so far. Eleven numbers in the span (558–560, 570, 575–576, 578–579, 616–618) have no photographed listings and no pages.

**The speckled openers (541–548, 561–564, 573).** Pale greige vessels peppered with [[sand-speckled|dark sand-like specks]], built in blocky shapes — cubes, blocks, collared jars, [[564]]'s turret with a window in its wall. Most carry their numbers [[numbered-bases|incised]] on little raised base pads. [[563]] interrupts the run with the catalog's first sentence: a flat plaque hand-lettered *Just a dream*.

**The dark silvered run (549–557).** Near-black crusted cups, blocks, and boxes with silvery patches weathering their walls — [[556]] banded like a hull, rust above and silver below. [[549]] and [[553]] hold [[glassy-pools|milky and silvery glossy pools]] in their dark floors.

**The rock pots (565–572).** Boulder, crater, mountain, rose, crag, and stump — dark lava-crusted forms at plum scale, [[567]] photographed on its plinth like a summit, [[568]] and [[569]] folding like two dark roses.

**The soil-brown dishes (577–612, with interruptions).** Coarse earth-brown dishes and plates — pressed-soil floors ([[588]] makes the penny look small), pie-crust lobes ([[589]]), swirled and brushed floors ([[602]], [[609]]), and [[600]] marking the series' exact \$6.00 milestone with a mud-crack floor. The black crumpled trio [[585]]–[[587]] and the glinting pair [[591]]/[[592]] cut through the run in matte black; the faceted rock-forms [[590]], [[599]], [[603]], and [[606]] give it its angles.

**The pale-base closers (613–619).** The exit ramp: [[613]] threads charcoal veins through bone-pale clay, then [[614]], [[615]], and [[619]] wear night-black over bare sand-buff bodies — the darkest surfaces in the batch, each hiding its palest face on the bottom.
