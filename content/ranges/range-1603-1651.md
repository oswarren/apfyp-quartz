Thirty-eight photographed pieces that swing through three clay looks in one stretch of the number line. Prices run \$16.03 to \$16.51; ten numbers in the span (1612, 1613, 1616–1620, 1626, 1633, 1642, 1650) have no photographed listing and no page.

**The white run (1603–1624).** Chalky white [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] bowls and pots with torn rims and glossy white-coated interiors. [[1614]] and [[1615]] carry their numbers [[numbered-bases|incised]] into their bases; [[1610]]'s torn rim breaks open to a red-orange underlayer — plaster-off-brick in miniature; [[1611]] mottles rust through the white. Fine interior cracks on [[1603]], [[1621]] and [[1624]] are recorded exactly as photographed, never as defects.

**The sandy interlude (1625–1630).** Unglazed-looking, sandstone-rough tan vessels: bowls and cups with crumbling rims, and two small-necked bottles, [[1628]] and [[1629]].

**The fissured red family (1631–1651).** Dusty plum, rose, and brick-red vessels whose sandy crusts craze into deep [[fissured-crust|fissure webs]] — several photographed outdoors standing on cracked orange ground that matches their skin. [[1639]] stacks its [[coil-ridges|coils]] like a turret; [[1646]] goes near-black charcoal; [[1641]] wears a pale ochre band like a stratum in cut earth.
