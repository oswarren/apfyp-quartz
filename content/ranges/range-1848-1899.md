Forty-eight pieces spanning a fifty-number block ($18.48–$18.99; 1861, 1866, 1869, and 1875 were never photographed and have no pages). Two families give the range its name. The **boulders** — closed, rock-like [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] vessels with small, torn, or crater mouths — run from [[1852]]'s chalk-veined stone to [[1879]]'s crater-mouthed orb and [[1891]]'s pierced rock. The **white linings** — dark, gritty cups that open onto bright, crazed white interiors — run from [[1876]] to [[1897]], with [[1894]]'s torn scalloped rim the most theatrical of them.

Around those two spines: a [[banded|zebra-banded]] pair ([[1888]], [[1890]]), [[white-threads|white thread-lines]] ([[1884]], [[1886]]), warm [[peach-patches|peach patches]] ([[1880]], [[1885]], [[1886]]), [[pale-wash|slate and granite washes]] ([[1855]], [[1856]], [[1871]], [[1874]]), the run's first strongly red piece ([[1883]]), a terracotta three-color cup ([[1895]]), a lichen-crusted one-off ([[1872]]), and a [[blue-flecks|blue-flecked]] closer ([[1899]]). The four openers 1848–1851 were photographed in the studio on a blue banding wheel — the only studio shots in the range.
