Fourteen dark [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] pieces photographed on the studio floor, most staged holding bundles of dried seed-heads. Prices run \$18.29 to \$18.44; two numbers in the span (1836, 1843) have no photographed listing and no page. The whole run reads like it was dug out of the ground — and it splits into two surface moods:

- **Frost over soil** — pale speckle scattered across dark clay, clearest on [[1829]], [[1832]] and [[1833]] (the [[frost-speckled|frost-speckled]] trio), with [[1830]]'s even sandy grain, [[1831]]'s sparser flecks, and [[1837]]'s white lace-mottle as its relatives.
- **Metal under lamplight** — graphite, copper, and silver lusters on [[1838]] through [[1841]], strongest on [[1840]], with [[1834]] and [[1839]] holding milky and amber [[glassy-pools|glossy pools]] at rim and crown.
