Forty-seven pieces ($19.00–$19.50), now including the lead-in 1900 just below the block; 1908, 1910, 1922, and 1938 remain reserve numbers with no pages. The range splits into two worlds. The dark first half continues the 1848–1899 mood — two [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] boulders ([[1901]], [[1905]]), a trio whose crusts split into [[rust-fissures|rust fissures]] ([[1902]], [[1904]], [[1911]]), five small cups with bubbled [[froth-interior|froth interiors]] ([[1909]] through [[1921]]), two [[white-interior|cream-lined]] cups ([[1913]], [[1923]]), and two bottles ([[1917]], [[1918]]) that finally gave [[1860]] its registry form. Above all it brings the catalog's **first lids**: nine lidded vessels, from pinched rust-black jars ([[1906]], [[1907]], [[1924]], [[1927]]) to a slab-built charcoal run — boulder jars ([[1945]], [[1948]]), a square box ([[1946]]), a hooded jar ([[1947]]), and a brick with a stone stopper ([[1949]]).

The pale second half is kin to [[range-2085-2092|the abstract cups]]: cream and white-buff cups under grey-lavender [[glazed-rim-band|rim bands]], decorated in dark [[brushed-glaze|brushwork]] — splatter ([[1925]], [[1926]]), horizon lines ([[1928]], [[1939]], [[1941]]), the dripping grey-rimmed pair [[1940]] and [[1942]] with [[glaze-flecks|green-and-red flecks]] in the pale glaze, dry scratch strokes ([[1929]]), one bold looping gesture ([[1943]]) — plus wrapped and scribbled lines whose method stays unproven ([[1931]], [[1934]]), leaf-print ghosts ([[1935]]), torn dark patches ([[1932]]), a recurring plum-drip note ([[1930]], [[1936]], [[1937]]), and two [[numbered-bases|handwritten base numbers]] ([[1925]], [[1944]]). A silver-banded footed pair ([[1915]], [[1916]]) and a rock-crusted closer ([[1950]]) bracket the seam between the worlds.
