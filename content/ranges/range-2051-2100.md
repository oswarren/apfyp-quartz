Forty-one pieces from $20.51 to $21.00, with three reserve gaps (2070, 2087, 2090) and the already-documented [[range-2085-2092|abstract cups]] sitting mid-range, untouched and linked from both sides. The run opens on brushwork carried over from the [[range-2001-2050|previous range]] — the toast-tan loop-handled [[2051]] and zebra-striped [[2052]] — then a milky pinched bowl ([[2053]]) and a stone-grey torn-rim bottle ([[2054]]) clear the table for the range's first family.

The rocky chawans hold the fifties: handle-less tea-bowl cups that photograph like river stones, six in a row from [[2055]] to [[2060]] plus kin at [[2062]], [[2063]], [[2064]], [[2066]], and [[2067]]. Three of them wear the [[pale-swipes|pale swipes]] that put a new term in the registry; four carry silvery line-drawings at their dark wells; three hold [[2062|glassy pools]]. In the middle of them sits the range's great oddity — [[2061]], the cup fused to a chip-studded rock — and past them, the white pair [[2068]] and [[2069]] jut their gnarled twisted handles sideways.

Then the vessels: nine large handbuilt forms, [[2071]] through [[2079]], from bare kneaded clay with a plum vein to the ten-photo speckle storm of [[2080]]. Six of them carry the batch's headline term — an [[erupted-nodule|erupted nodule]], boss, or shard plate breaking through the wall — from [[2073]]'s foamy burst to the shard-swallowing jar [[2077]]. After the frozen abstract cups, the range runs home through the painted ink-wash family ([[2081]]–[[2084]], photographed at a home table, group portraits and all), the sandy white-lined twins [[2096]] and [[2097]], and the splashed tail of navy-blob cups — [[2093]], [[2094]], [[2095]], [[2098]], and the frosted indigo [[2099]] — closing at the century mark: [[2100]], twenty-one dollars even.
