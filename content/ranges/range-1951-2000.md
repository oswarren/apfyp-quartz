Fifty consecutive pieces with no reserve gaps — the first full house in the reviewed catalog ($19.51–$20.00). The run opens white: cups veined, smeared, and streaked with dark grit over pale glaze ([[1951]], [[1957]], [[1977]]), two pinholed cream cups ([[1955]], [[1956]]), a silvery line-drawing hiding inside [[1952]], and a torn-rim cup wearing its [[1953|hairline crack]] openly. Then the ground turns to stone: granite chawans ([[1958]], [[1959]], [[1962]]), one of which — [[1963]] — pairs a store-stated "Leaks!" with the run's best secret, a [[1963|marbled two-clay interior]].

The middle is the boulder decade: nine closed rock-forms from [[1960]] (pink-blushed, noduled, number painted on the flank) through the burnt-and-snow faces of [[1961]], the lavender-staged [[1964]] and [[1968]], to the [[leaf-print|leaf-print]] fossil [[1967]] and the tall cratered [[1972]]. After them, the tea line returns wearing the run's blue thread — milky blue-grey [[glazed-rim-band|rim bands]] on [[1973]], [[1989]] and [[1990]] — past the lettered stone [[1979]], the [[glassy-pools|white pool]] at [[1982]]'s well, and the [[lichen-crust|mud-crack crust]] of [[1983]].

And then the catalog does something it had never done: it grows [[pulled-handle|**handles**]]. Six mugs ([[1954]], [[1971]], [[1986]], [[1994]], [[1996]], [[1998]]), two two-eared cups ([[1992]], [[1993]]), and four two-handled amphora-form vessels — [[1995]], [[1997]], [[1999]] and the pale-streaked millennium closer [[2000]] — twelve handled pieces where the first 1,953 had none.
