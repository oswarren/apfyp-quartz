Eleven photographed pieces sitting between two documented neighbors — [[range-1692-1714|the wild-clay stretch]] ends at 1714, [[range-1727-1769|the black clay run]] begins at 1727, and this short range bridges the gap, picking up the lead-in 1715 just below its span. Prices run \$17.15 to \$17.25; the span is now fully photographed, with no reserve gaps.

The whole range is [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] and dark, and it splits into a few kinds of photograph:

- **Studio close-ups** — [[1716]], a crusted jar studded with [[embedded-particles|pale angular inclusions]], and [[1718]], whose surface glints pewter-bronze in full [[metallic-sheen|metallic sheen]]. Both photographed in the workshop, torn rims and all.
- **One group portrait** — [[1722]], [[1723]], [[1724]] and [[1725]] share a single listing photo: four dark vessels in a row on a stone ledge, left to right in number order. Each page describes its own position in the line.
- **Bronze boulders** — three rock-form vessels, [[1719]], [[1720]] and [[1721]], glinting dark [[metallic-sheen|pewter-bronze]], their bases ringed with pale [[base-patches|sage-and-gold patches]]. [[1715]]'s crusted column and [[1717]]'s glaze-rimmed tumbler round out the range.
