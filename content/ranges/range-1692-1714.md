Twenty-two photographed pieces of rough, handbuilt vessels whose store titles keep saying the same words: wild clay, red clay, local clay. Every piece's price is its number times one cent, so this stretch runs from \$16.92 to \$17.14. One number inside the span (1696) has no photographed listing and no page.

The whole stretch is [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] — pinched, coiled, torn at the rim — and most of it photographs terracotta red. Within that family, the photos show several distinct looks:

- **Metal-like glint** — a pewter [[metallic-sheen|metallic sheen]], cause never asserted: [[1692]], [[1697]] (with a glassy amber rim band), [[1706]] (the strongest)
- **Coils left visible** — stacked [[coil-ridges|coil ridges]] climbing the wall: [[1699]], [[1701]] (softer seams on [[1698]], [[1700]], [[1708]] stay in prose)
- **Glossy black studding** — protruding [[surface-nodules|surface nodules]] the store calls "Lake Erie Inclusions": [[1702]], [[1703]]
- **Pale specks in the body** — [[embedded-particles|embedded particles]]: [[1694]], [[1697]], [[1710]]
- **White crusty deposits** — heavy on [[1714]], lighter on [[1712]]; what they are, the photos don't say
- **A number in the clay** — [[1697]] carries its number [[numbered-bases|incised]] in its base
- **Colors off the main red** — [[1694]] photographs dusty mauve, [[1709]] dusty rose-brown, [[1710]] charcoal-dark, [[1692]] and [[1706]] deep brown; those pages describe the color without naming a color tag
