Fifty-eight pieces from $17.70 to $18.28, with a single reserve gap at 1821. The range opens where the [[range-1727-1769|previous one]] left off but changes register completely: after the pale terracotta and buff cups of the 1700s, this block is almost entirely **dark** — charcoal, near-black, and grey-brown [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] vessels photographed across August and September 2025, their surfaces dusted, crazed, or crusted rather than glazed, many still reading raw and rocky.

It begins with the block's signature oddity: a family of small charcoal cups with one or two round holes bored clean **through** their walls ([[1770]]–[[1778]]), their dark bodies scattered with pale [[frost-speckled|frost-like speckle]]; the first is photographed with a green reed slid through its openings. From there the forms loosen — a waisted pale-marbled vessel ([[1779]]), a tan-patched column ([[1780]]) — into a run of crazed [[lichen-crust|pale-crusted]] cups ([[1781]], [[1782]], [[1793]], [[1800]]), one of them pressed with a square grid ([[1782]]), and a trio of cups flecked with small **green and yellow** glassy specks ([[1783]], [[1784]], [[1785]]).

A sandy, gritty family threads through the middle: bare [[sand-grit|sand-textured]] cups and vessels ([[1788]], [[1789]], [[1802]], [[1804]], [[1806]], [[1826]]), several signing their bare bases with an [[numbered-bases|incised or painted number]]. The largest pieces are closed rock-form **boulders** ([[1790]], [[1792]], [[1802]], [[1812]], [[1814]], [[1815]], [[1816]], [[1825]]) — one wearing a pewter [[metallic-sheen|metallic sheen]] ([[1816]]), one webbed in pale [[lichen-crust|crust]] ([[1815]]) — and two near-black columns hold pale grog-like [[embedded-particles|particles]] in the clay ([[1808]], [[1809]]).

The range closes on its crustiest and most finished-looking surfaces: two cups thick with a pale [[beaded-crust|beaded crust]] ([[1817]], [[1818]]), a pair of flaring vessels with painted base numbers ([[1819]], [[1820]]), a milky white glaze running down the wall of a tumbler and a boulder ([[1822]], [[1825]]), and finally a torn-necked charcoal **bottle** frosted all over ([[1828]]), which links forward into [[1829]]. The photos span roughly six weeks — August 2 to September 15, 2025 — with a few later store screenshots carrying no capture date ([[1799]], [[1800]], [[1801]]); every listing photo in the range was reviewed up close on 2026-07-13.
