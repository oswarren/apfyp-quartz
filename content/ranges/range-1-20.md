Where the ledger starts. Sixteen photographed pieces from the first twenty numbers of the series — and because every piece's price is its number times one cent, these are the cheapest pieces there will ever be: \$0.01 to \$0.20, the whole range together costing less than a cup of coffee. Four numbers in the span (4, 6, 7, 9) have no photographed listing and no page.

These are miniatures. [[2|Piece 2]] and [[5|piece 5]] were photographed outdoors beside a US penny — a coin that is both their scale reference and, near enough, their price tag — on March 22 and 23, 2023, the two oldest photography dates in the whole catalog. The rest carry small, plain photos on concrete, mostly of charcoal-dark vessels:

- **Construction left visible** — stacked [[coil-ridges|coil rings]] climbing the wall: [[11]], [[15]] (softer bands on [[8]], [[13]], [[17]], [[20]] stay in prose)
- **Charcoal-dark bodies** — [[3]], [[8]], [[11]], [[14]], [[15]], [[19]] photograph near-black; the color is named as a color, nothing more
- **Small open forms** — bowls [[3]], [[12]], [[16]], the dish [[10]], the cup [[19]]
- **Odd ones out** — the spiral-grooved disc [[2]], the narrow-necked bottle [[14]], the closed banded tower [[20]], and [[1|piece 1]] itself, the only pale piece of the group
