Thirty-four photographed pieces carrying the red run of the low 1600s to its close. Prices run \$16.53 to \$16.87; one number in the span (1675) has no photographed listing and no page.

**The sandy red openers (1653–1658).** Pink-red and rose vessels with coarse, crack-webbed crusts, photographed in the workshop — on a blue turntable, held in hand before a table crowded with the series itself. [[1655]] carries its number [[numbered-bases|incised]] in its base; [[1657]]'s rim rises in a skyline of points.

**Dark interruptions (1659–1660).** A dark crackled pot with base digits too rough to read, then the first of the crowned ones: [[1660]], whose sunken top carries [[glassy-pools|glossy, glass-like pools]] around a porthole mouth — the newest term in the registry, with three members here.

**The torn-crown reds (1661–1678).** Brick-red cups, cylinders, and pots with torn, scalloped, castellated crowns — [[1674]]'s rounded castle merlons, [[1677]]'s outward-folding flaps, [[1672]]'s saw-tooth edge. [[1663]] and the stepped [[1683]] wear their [[coil-ridges|coil bands]] plainly. Mixed in: the pale-dusted plum pieces — [[1664]] and [[1665]] under a [[pale-wash|sage-cream dusting]] — and [[1676]], the run's one sand-buff interruption. [[1673]] goes near-black with a [[metallic-sheen|pewter sheen]], answered later by the gunmetal bottle [[1680]] and its triangular mouth.

**The windowsill series (1681–1687).** Sunlit sill, a twine-tied bundle of dried plants, a dark rock for company: maroon and red cups and vases photographed in warm light, ending with [[1679]]'s milky pooled crown just before it and [[1687]] — the tall marbled vase photographed actually holding the bundle, the range's closing image.
