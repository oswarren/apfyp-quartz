Fifty consecutive pieces with no reserve gaps — the second full house in a row ($20.01–$20.50). The third thousand opens rusted: a pale boulder with a [[rust-veil|rust-webbed crown]] ([[2001]]), then the family spreads across a necked jar ([[2004]]), a chimney-necked bottle ([[2009]]), a plum-capped pear ([[2027]]), the brick-red wash of [[2028]], the nearly all-red plates of [[2035]], and a crazed pink flank on [[2036]] — seven pieces that put a new term in the registry.

The middle of the run belongs to marks and stone. Deliberate [[scored-marks|incised marks]] return after a 250-piece silence — squiggles and hatching on the glazed jar [[2010]], loop-and-grid on [[2012]], dashes across [[2014]] — while two pieces carry half-buried incised *words*, quoted and never interpreted ([[2011]], [[2013]]). Seven [[2034|amphoras]] raise their ears through the twenties and thirties, boulders take the batch by number — twenty-three of them, from [[2025]]'s ripple ridges to [[2038]]'s bark swirls — and the [[lichen-crust|mud-crack crust]] family doubles: warm-ground plates on [[2016]] and the eleven-photo monument [[2039]], blue-suffused panels on [[2030]], flaking white rosettes on near-black [[2040]].

Then the batch goes blue. Small glassy [[blue-flecks|blue flecks]], pools, and beads surface on seven pieces — hiding in a crevice on [[2035]], veining [[2034]]'s neck — before the color takes whole summits: the lapis dome of [[2037]], and the workshop-sunlit iridescent crowns of [[2041]] and [[2042]], the three founding members of [[blue-crust|blue-crust]]. The lone open form of the fifty, pinched bowl [[2043]], resets the table for the finale: seven cups closing the range, from the sister pair [[2044]] and [[2045]] with their [[numbered-bases|incised base numbers]] to the [[brushed-glaze|brushwork]] run of [[2047]], [[2048]], [[2049]], and [[2050]] — bold strokes, a framed patchwork panel, and a caramel closer that wears the batch's number fifty in its own base.
