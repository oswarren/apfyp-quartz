![Bare sand-textured handbuilt ceramic sipping cup with a rust-brown band and a glossy white glazed rim, piece 2096 of A Penny For Your Pottery.](https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0628/8434/8065/files/PXL-20260421_071726036.jpg?v=1777563974)

A hard turn from the painted cups around it: 2096 is bare sand outside — warm tan-grey, gritty with white specks and fine dark flecks, a rust-brown toasted band circling the lower body — and wet white glass inside, where the rim rolls over into a fully glazed glossy liner. The two surfaces meet in a clean-ish line at the rim like a beach meeting surf. Its base holds an inset recessed medallion of smoother clay with [[numbered-bases|"2096" scrawled]] into it, incised without ink. Its sandy twin is next door at [[2097]].

**Why is the outside rough and the inside glass?** Grip and sip, respectively — sand for the hand, gloss for the mouth, one rim roll between them. Piece 2096 of 10,000, listed at $20.96.
