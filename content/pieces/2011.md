Six photos circle a bottle that looks pulled from a shipwreck: a [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] ovoid rising to a true neck with a flared, wavy trumpet rim, coppery-brown glaze pooling glassy inside it. Crusted deposits in amber, grey, and brown grip the shoulders, ruffled fin-like ears stand at either side — solid, not loops — and a small hole pierces one shoulder. Mid-flank, someone incised letters into the wet clay; the glaze then buried half of them. They start like "Woo—" and end in speculation.

**What does the word on piece 2011 say?** The photos show three letters clearly and swallow the rest — Wood? Wool? Woo? The page reports the scratch, not the sentence. Piece 2011 of 10,000, listed at $20.11.
