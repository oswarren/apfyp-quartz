The run opens loud: a [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] cup whose glossy white glaze is torn through by bold, sand-textured [[dark-smears|dark veins]] — grey-brown swaths that ride the wall like exposed rock under snow. The rim pinches and dips; the interior pools glossy white with a faint ring at the well; thin lines wander the white zones and are left to be what they are. What the dark grit is — body, slip, something the fire did — the photos don't say, so this page doesn't either.

**Is the dark part under or over the white?** Both, to the eye — the veins sit proud and sandy where the white thins, which is exactly as much as two photos can prove. Piece 1951 of 10,000, listed at $19.51 — the first page of [[range-1951-2000|the 1951–2000 run]].
