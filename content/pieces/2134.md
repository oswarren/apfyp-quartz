![Ceramic cup washed nearly whole in watery steel-blue with a white cloud window holding black strokes, piece 2134 of A Penny For Your Pottery.](https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0628/8434/8065/files/PXL-20260421_233318507.jpg?v=1779295521)

The bluest piece of the range: a watery steel-blue wash covers almost the whole cup, drag-mottled and translucent, with white reserved only as a collar at the rim and one cloud-window at mid-body where a group of angular black strokes sits. Faint throwing ridges show through the blue; the flared foot pools darker; even the base disc is crossed by a broad blue band with [[numbered-bases|"2134" handwritten]] in black over it.

**Is any of it unpainted?** The rim collar, the window, and not much else — even the underside got a stroke of blue before the number went on. Piece 2134 of 10,000, listed at $21.34.
