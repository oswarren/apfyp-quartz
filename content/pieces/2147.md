The sandier sibling of [[2146]]: same warm-white body and buried grain, but here the sand rises to the surface as a broad sandy-taupe band wrapping the whole upper body, granular and dry-looking, ending in an uneven waterline above bare white. One photo puts the cup in the maker's hand. Under the turned foot, [[numbered-bases|"2147" handwritten]] sits in faint dry brown, its tail smeared sideways — the digits still read, the smear just travels with them.

**Where does the sand stop?** At a wavering line about two-thirds down, sharp on one flank and feathered on the other — the band was applied to end there, and the ending shows its hand. Piece 2147 of 10,000, listed at $21.47.
