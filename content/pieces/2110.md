![White handbuilt ceramic cup with navy blots, an ice-blue column, and a navy stroke crossing the rim into the interior, piece 2110 of A Penny For Your Pottery.](https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0628/8434/8065/files/PXL-20260421_073206949.jpg?v=1778261439)

Second of the white splash cups, and the first where the paint refuses to stop at the rim: one navy stroke climbs the wall, crosses the lip, and keeps going down into the white interior. Around it, navy blots (one pocked with pinholes), a cold ice-blue column, and an umber sponge-cloud share the walls of this [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] cup. The base shows [[numbered-bases|"2110" handwritten]] on bare clay.

**How far inside does the stroke go?** A finger-length past the rim, tapering as it descends — the interior's only mark. Piece 2110 of 10,000, listed at $21.10.
