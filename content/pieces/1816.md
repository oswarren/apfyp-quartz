A near-spherical, [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] charcoal boulder — closed and rock-like, opening only at a torn crater mouth on top, no neck and no handles. The wall is deeply cratered and pocked, and across the sunlit photos its raised ridges catch a cool [[metallic-sheen|pewter luster]] while the hollows stay dark. It is staged on a round table in a wood-panelled workshop, a window and electrical panels behind.

**What makes 1816 catch the light like that?** In the sunlit shots the high points of its cratered wall carry a pewter sheen that tracks the form as the angle changes — salt-fired, the luster the salt firing left. Piece 1816 of 10,000, listed at $18.16.
