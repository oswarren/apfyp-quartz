The batch's biggest photo set — eleven shots circling a monumental, urn-scale boulder from every side. The swirled warm-brown marbled body rides twin shoulder humps down to a narrow trimmed foot, with charcoal-black smudge zones on the bare clay and a small torn star of a mouth at the crown. The defining feature is white: flat, eggshell-like [[lichen-crust|mud-crack crust panels]] cap the rim on one side and wash across the shoulders and flanks, webbed with crazing over the warm ground. Look close and tiny white glassy beads sit in the surface pits, black drip lines run vertically down the back, and the whole [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] skin is pocked and gritty.

**Why eleven photos for piece 2039?** It's the kind of piece you walk around — every quarter-turn moves the white panels, the smudges, and the drips into a new arrangement. Piece 2039 of 10,000, listed at $20.39.
