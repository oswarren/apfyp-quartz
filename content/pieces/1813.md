A [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] bottle: a rounded body that draws in at the shoulder and rises to a short, torn neck with a small open mouth, no handles. The [[bare-clay|unglazed]] dark brown-black wall is gritty and rough, mottled all over with a pale dusty bloom and a few raised white crusty patches. The first photo holds it up to a dirty window with greenery beyond, so light passes around the silhouette; the others set it on the sill.

**What makes 1813 a bottle rather than a boulder?** It has a distinct short neck — the shoulders pull in and lift to a torn collar and small mouth — where the closed boulders in this run have no neck at all. Piece 1813 of 10,000, listed at $18.13.
