The batch's fossil: a near-black [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] boulder wearing a full [[leaf-print|leaf impression]] — lobed like an oak, veins intact — pressed pale-tan into the crust, with smaller leaf ghosts breaking through around it and leopard-spots of dark showing where the leaf skeleton opened. It is the second leaf print the reviewed catalog has seen, after [[1935]]'s ghosted prints on a pale cup, and by far the clearer of the pair. One frame stands a dried lavender sprig in the mouth — staging, noted as such.

**Did a real leaf make this?** The veining argues yes; the page records the argument and leaves the verdict to the pot. Piece 1967 of 10,000, listed at $19.67.
