A chalky white cup with a handle that refuses to behave: a gnarled, visibly twisted [[pulled-handle|pulled handle]] jutting straight out to the side like a little pan grip, its blunt end torn rather than tucked. The rim and upper body run glossy white; the lower body goes matte and crackled. One flank opens a small star-shaped fissure in the glaze — recorded here exactly as photographed. Inside, the greige matte well holds glossy blob traces. The base carries ghost-faint pressed digits this page declines to read. Its twin, [[2069]], wears the same white body and the same sideways twist.

**Why does the handle stick out sideways?** Because it was pulled, twisted, and left exactly where the maker's hand stopped — one of two cups in the range that carry the gesture. Piece 2068 of 10,000, listed at $20.68.
