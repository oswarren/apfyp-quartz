The chawan run turns deliberate. Held in a hand, this cream tea bowl carries clear [[brushed-glaze|brushwork]]: navy-black vertical bars crossed by horizontal strokes, drawn firmly enough to read as a gate or a grid against a pale-blue wash, with rust warming the clay below. Where the earlier chawans washed and splashed, this one is composed of discrete, confident strokes. The base carries [[numbered-bases|"2184" handwritten]] over throwing rings.

**Is that a symbol?** It falls into a gate-like lattice of bars, but it's recorded as brushwork — deliberate navy strokes, never read as a letter or character. Piece 2184 of 10,000, listed at $21.84.
