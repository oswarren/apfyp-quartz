The writing cup. A globe-bodied cup wears a coarse, sandy umber-brown wash over white, with a matte black band riding the shoulder — and someone has [[incised-word|scratched words clean through the coat]] while it could still be cut, the lines showing white against the brown. The legible fragments, kept separate because the cup keeps them separate: "I'm going- -to", "Home", "That", and a trailing "...!!!". Between them, doodles — boxes, a shape that could be an eye or a boat, a round-headed stick figure. What it adds up to, the cup doesn't say. The base carries ribbed grooves and [[numbered-bases|"2141" handwritten]] in brown.
