The first piece of [[range-1901-1950|the 1901–1950 run]] carries batch 9's boulder family straight across the boundary: a [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] closed orb with a small hole at the crown, washed in marbled cream that pools into pearlescent drips down the shoulder. The wash swirls rather than covers — darker body shows through in soft veins, so the whole rock reads streaked, like weathered marble.

**Is it a vase?** Only just — the opening at the top is a small hole rather than a mouth, which is what makes it a boulder in this catalog's vocabulary. Piece 1901 of 10,000, listed at $19.01.
