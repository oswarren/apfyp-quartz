The odd one out. Set among painted tea bowls, this cup wears no blue or black at all: a bare warm grey-beige body, rough and [[sand-grit|sand-textured]], with dark grains and pale specks visible right in the surface — and, pouring over the rim, a glossy white glazed liner that fills the inside. Sand for the hand, glass for the mouth, exactly the contrast that named the family back on cups [[2096]] and [[2103]]. The base carries [[numbered-bases|"2188" handwritten]] on the gritty clay.

**Why no color here?** Because this cup keeps its clay bare — a grit-flecked stoneware shell around a smooth white liner, the sand-grit look dropped in among the painted chawans. Piece 2188 of 10,000, listed at $21.88.
