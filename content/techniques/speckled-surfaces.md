**What is a speckled surface?** Speckling is the scatter of small dark spots across a ceramic surface. In general ceramics terms, specks like these come from iron- or manganese-bearing particles in or under the glaze that melt and bloom during firing — each speck is a tiny mineral event. The result can't be drawn on purpose, spot by spot; the material decides where the dots land.

This site describes what the listing photos show and doesn't state the specific clay or glaze composition of individual pieces.

## How to spot it — and how it varies

The interesting thing about speckling in this series is that the *pattern* differs piece to piece, even within one glaze family:

- **Washes** — speckling concentrated toward the lower body, fading upward ([[2250]], [[2252]])
- **Bands** — speckling gathered in a belt around the body ([[2257]], [[2260]])
- **Clusters** — scattered, star-like groupings ([[2255]])
- **Dense fields** — heavy speckling across the whole surface, with visible coarse particles ([[2259]])

Two pieces with the same glaze and the same numbers-apart can wear completely different constellations — one more way each numbered piece ends up unrepeatable.
