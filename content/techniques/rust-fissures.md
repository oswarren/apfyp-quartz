**What are the red cracks?** On three dark vessels in [[range-1901-1950|pieces 1901–1950]], a crusted band or zone splits into fissures, and the splits open onto rust-red to plum tones underneath — like dried mud cracking over embers. [[1902]] carries the seams across a dark crust band; [[1904]] cracks wider; [[1911]] shows the look best across three photos.

The term names the look — rust-red to plum tones opening in the splits — not the cause beneath. The fissures are observed, never characterized as damage. A different fissured look has its own term: [[fissured-crust|fissured crust]] webs a sandy body with nothing revealed beneath.
