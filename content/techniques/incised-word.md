**What do the pots say?** Three reviewed pieces carry words cut into their clay while it was still soft. [[2011]]'s trumpet bottle shows letters starting like "Woo—" before the glaze buries the rest; [[2013]]'s dark amphora says **SUP**, plainly; and [[2141]] — the writing cup — carries whole fragments scratched through its sandy brown coat: "I'm going- -to", "Home", "That", and a trailing "...!!!", with doodles between them.

The rule on every page is the same: the letters are quoted exactly as they photograph and never interpreted, completed, or assembled into a message. A half-buried word stays half-buried; 2141's fragments are never joined into a sentence. The term is kept distinct from [[glaze-text|glaze text]], whose words are written in glaze decoration rather than cut into the clay. Held at the 2026-07-12 batch-12 ruling with two members, approved in the batch-14 ruling when 2141 made three.
