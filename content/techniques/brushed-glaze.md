**What's painted on these cups?** Brushed decoration — slip, glaze, or mason stain depending on the piece — applied by hand with a brush: bold black patches, thin sweeping lines, soft blue watercolor-like washes, flicked splatter. Unlike a dipped or poured coat that covers a piece evenly, brushwork keeps the gesture: you can see stroke direction, thin spots where the ground shows through, and edges where one color rides over another.

Four of the [[range-2085-2092|abstract cups]] carry it: [[2086]] (black patches and blue washes), [[2089]] (a blue marbled rim band over a washy ground), [[2091]] (black patches, one thin blue line, blue splatter), and [[2092]] (brushwork that turns into [[glaze-text|handwritten words]]).
