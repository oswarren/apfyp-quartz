**Why is the whole top of the pot blue?** Three reviewed pieces from the 2001–2050 range carry a vivid ultramarine-to-cobalt granular crust across broad fields — not flecks, whole territories. On [[2037]] it coats the entire crown like lapis, mixed with white grit, with blue-grey drips escaping down the shoulders; on [[2041]] and [[2042]] it pools and patches around tiny crown mouths among white glassy beads and foam blobs, flashing iridescent in the workshop sun the studio photos caught.

The registry keeps this apart from [[blue-flecks|blue-flecks]], which names *small* blue glassy flecks and pools — broad matte coverage was ruled out of that term twice (pieces 2030 and 2037) before this one existed. What the blue is — glaze, slip, mineral — is never asserted. Approved in the 2026-07-12 batch-12 ruling with all three members arriving in one batch.
