**Why does the inside look like foam?** Five small dark cups in [[range-1901-1950|pieces 1901–1950]] are lined with a frothy, bubbled cream-to-brown coating that laps up and over their rims — in the macros it reads like sea foam caught mid-fizz, sometimes breaking into leopard-spotted patches. [[1912]] and [[1919]] show the spotting most clearly; [[1909]], [[1920]] and [[1921]] wear quieter versions of the same boil.

What bubbled — glaze, slip, ash, something else in the fire — is exactly what a photo can't prove, so the pages describe the look and leave the material alone. Related-but-different looks stay separate: [[1918]]'s frothy bottle neck is described in prose on its own page, and smooth glassy pooled looks carry the registry's glassy-pools term instead.
