**Why does the handle have beads on it?** Two reviewed cups carry [[pulled-handle|pulled handles]] whose spines are studded with dark glossy beads or nubs — a row of them riding the arc of [[2006]]'s pierced handle, and glossy blobs seated at [[2020]]'s knotted handle joins. The effect reads like rivets, or dew on a stem.

What the studs are — beads of glaze, embedded stone, deliberate nubs of darker clay — is never asserted; the pages record where they sit and how they shine. Approved in the 2026-07-12 batch-12 ruling, entering the registry at exactly the 2-piece minimum.
