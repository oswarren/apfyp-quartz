**What makes the stripes?** Two cups in [[range-1848-1899|pieces 1848–1899]] wear broad, wavy bands of pale buff and near-black wrapping the full body — a zebra look rather than a single dipped line. On [[1888]] the buff zones are sandy and crusted, split by an open crack from the rim; on [[1890]] cream and black alternate all the way down while the interior goes whitewash-pale.

The photos show the pattern, not the process — whether the bands are layered clays, slips, or something else is never asserted. Single-band, two-tone pieces are a different look and stay in prose: the "dipped horizon" of [[1868]], the dripping grey shoulder of [[1880]], and the cracked cream band of [[1889]] are each described on their own pages, untagged.
