**Why is there blue on a bare black pot?** Two pieces in the black stoneware run carry small glassy blue marks — sparse scattered flecks on [[1750]], and on [[1751]] a distinct blue patch near the rim with flecks trailing across the body. They catch light the way glass does, and the store's title for 1751 calls them blue glaze inclusions; the pages quote that without asserting what melted where.

Against the matte dark clay the effect is tiny but loud — the only color event in a colorless run.
