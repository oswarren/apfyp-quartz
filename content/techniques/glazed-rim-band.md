**What is a glazed rim band?** A stripe of glaze along the rim of an otherwise [[bare-clay|bare]] pot, usually running down into the interior — the drinking edge gets the glass, the body stays raw. Seven pieces around [[range-1571-1597|1571–1597]] wear it: gray bands over red clay on [[1571]], [[1574]], and [[1578]], over sandy buff on [[1576]] and [[1577]], over marbled bodies on [[1579]] and [[1580]].

The contrast line where glaze stops and clay starts is different on every one.
