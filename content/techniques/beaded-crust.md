**Why does the cup look like a rock?** Five reviewed cups wear a thick pale crust built up over the whole exterior — knobbly, beaded, and encrusted, cracked and lumped like weathered stone, with dark bits and warm iron stains caught in the pale field — while the inside stays a smooth, glossy glazed pool. The contrast is the signature: a rough mineral shell for the hand, a clean liner for the drink. The five members arrived together across two June photo sessions: [[2245]] closes the June-08 group, and [[2246]], [[2247]], [[2248]], and [[2249]] carry the look through June-16 up to the frozen pilot's edge.

What the crust is made of, and how it was built up, is never asserted — the pages describe only how it photographs, following the same caution as the crazed [[lichen-crust|lichen crust]] and the bare [[sand-grit|sand grit]]; per the catalog's condition discipline, its cracks and lumps are a surface look, never called flaking, failing, or a defect. Approved as a five-member family in the 2026-07-13 batch-16 ruling.
