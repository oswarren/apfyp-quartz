Every piece in this series is handmade, and the surfaces, marks, and small irregularities are where that shows. These pages explain the recurring techniques and details you'll see across the catalog — what each one is, why it happens, and which pieces to look at.

- [[handbuilding|Handbuilding]] — shaped by hand, not thrown; why no two rims match
- [[crackle-glaze|Crackle glaze]] — the fine web of lines in the glaze surface
- [[speckled-surfaces|Speckled surfaces]] — mineral speckling in washes, bands, and clusters
- [[numbered-bases|Numbered bases]] — the hand-written number that is both identity and price

Every concept here is also a browsable tag — each page above links to the full list of pieces that carry it, and every piece page links back to the ideas visible in its photos. Buying always happens on [the Shopify store](https://apennyforyourpottery.com).

More pages are added as details recur across reviewed pieces — a detail earns a page here once it's documented on at least two pieces, or when it's central enough to the project to explain on its own.
