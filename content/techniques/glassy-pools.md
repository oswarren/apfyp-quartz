**What's the shiny stuff on top of these pots?** On a handful of the dark vessels around [[range-1653-1687|pieces 1653–1687]], the crown carries glossy, glass-like pools — bright material settled into the hollows and dripping into the crevices, vivid against a matte, sandy body. On [[1660]] the pools ring a small porthole of a mouth; on [[1679]] they web the entire crown in milky white, the clearest case in the catalog; on [[1684]] they stop mid-wall, a glassy crown over a bare brick-red base. Lower on the number line the pools move indoors: [[549]] holds a milky grey-white pool in its dark floor, and [[553]] a silvery one — interior cases, same rule.

A photo can show where the gloss sits and how it shines — it can't prove what melted to make it. Glaze, ash, or something else entirely: the pages describe the look and leave the recipe alone. That's the rule for the tag, too — it marks only clear pooled cases; a general shine or scattered gloss stays in prose.
