**What are the thin white lines?** On two dark bellied vessels in [[range-1848-1899|pieces 1848–1899]], thin white lines wrap the body like loose threads — some run near-horizontal, some cross at a diagonal, all of them fine as string against the near-black ground. [[1884]] carries them almost alone; [[1886]] tangles them with [[peach-patches|peach patches]] and a grey drippy rim band.

How the lines got there — trailed slip, glaze, a scratch through to something pale — is what a photo can't prove, so the pages describe the look and leave the method alone. Fainter line work stays in prose: [[1877]]'s thin white bands are described on its own page, untagged.
