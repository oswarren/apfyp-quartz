**What does "bare clay" mean here?** No glaze — the fired clay body itself is the finished surface. On a glazed pot you see a glass layer; on these, you see the material: matte, granular, marked by every pinch and fold of [[handbuilding|the making]]. Most of the black stoneware run ([[range-1727-1769|pieces 1727–1769]]) is bare, and so are the white-buff and marbled vessels around [[range-1571-1597|1571–1597]]. Bare surfaces carry detail glaze would soften — [[embedded-particles|pale specks]], [[surface-nodules|protruding grit]], [[fabric-impression|cloth imprints]], [[scored-marks|scored lines]].

These pages describe what the listing photos show and don't state clay composition or firing method.
