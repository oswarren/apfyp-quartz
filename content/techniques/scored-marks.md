**What are the scratch marks on 1756?** Deliberate-looking scored lines — hatched scratches cut into the clay across the whole body of [[1756]], an egg-shaped closed form with a small opening at the top. The marks cross and overlap like combing, turning the surface into texture you can read from arm's length. What tool made them, the photos don't show, so the pages don't name one.

Fainter cousins appear elsewhere in the run — light striations on [[1744]], drag lines on [[1767]] — described on those pages, but only 1756 wears it as its defining feature.
