**Why do these cups have holes through them?** Nine dark charcoal cups, photographed together on one August 2025 day, each carry one or more round holes bored clean through the wall — open from one side straight through to the other. In the first cup's listing a green reed is slid through the openings; across the family the holes are the shared signature, small round apertures pierced through pinched, torn-rimmed [[handbuilding|handbuilt]] cups dusted with pale [[frost-speckled|frost-like speckle]].

The catalog describes the holes only as they photograph — round openings pierced through the wall — and does not assert what they are for or how they were made; per the same caution the pierced walls are never called functional or watertight either way. Approved as a nine-member family in the 2026-07-13 batch-17 ruling.
