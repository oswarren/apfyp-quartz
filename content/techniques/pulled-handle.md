**Where did the handles come from?** For 1,953 pieces the reviewed catalog showed none. Then [[range-1951-2000|the 1951–2000 run]] grew twelve: six mugs with a single rough loop ([[1954]], [[1971]], [[1986]], [[1994]], [[1996]], [[1998]]), two cups with an ear on each side ([[1992]], [[1993]]), and four two-handled amphora-form vessels ([[1995]], [[1997]], [[1999]], [[2000]]). Each loop is pulled and attached by hand — pinch marks and join seams left showing, some pierced wide, some twisted like rope.

The term records that a handle is *there*, as photographed. Strength, comfort, and whether a finger fits are things a photo can't testify to, so no page claims them.
