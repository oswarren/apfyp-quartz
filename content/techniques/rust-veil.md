**Why do so many pale pieces look rusted?** Seven reviewed pieces in the 2001–2050 range wear a granular, crumbly rust-red to plum-maroon crust over a pale body — sometimes webbing the crown ([[2001]]), sometimes capping it ([[2027]]), sometimes washing halfway down ([[2028]]), sometimes wrapping nearly the whole form in plates ([[2035]]), and once crazing a single flank patch ([[2036]]). [[2004]] and [[2009]] carry the quieter mottled versions that started the family.

What the crust is — slip, glaze, iron blooming in the fire — is never asserted; the pages record the look and leave the recipe to the kiln. The term is kept apart from [[rust-fissures|rust-fissures]], where rust shows through splits in a *dark* body's crust: rust-veil sits *on* a pale one. Warm-bodied cousins like [[2015]] and [[2034]] stay in prose, outside the definition's pale-body line. Approved in the 2026-07-12 batch-12 ruling after the family grew from three members to seven across a single batch.
