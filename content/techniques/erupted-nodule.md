**What's bursting out of these vessels?** Six reviewed pieces in the 2051–2100 range — all from one run of large handbuilt vessels — carry something erupting through the wall: a foamy pale boss on [[2073]], twin maroon glassy nodules on [[2074]], a crumbly red cluster on [[2075]], shard-like plates ringed with crack halos all over the glazed jar [[2077]] and the bare globe [[2078]], and a pocked patch trailing a drip down [[2079]].

What erupted — grog bursting, a stone inclusion, a mass added on purpose — is never asserted; the pages record the breach and leave the geology alone. The term is kept apart from [[surface-nodules|surface-nodules]], whose small dark nodules ride *on* the surface: an erupted nodule comes *through* it. Neighbors that almost qualify stay in prose — [[2076]]'s red flecks are pressed in, not breaking out, and [[2080]]'s pale patches smear over its speckle rather than erupting. Approved in the 2026-07-12 batch-13 ruling with six founding members.
