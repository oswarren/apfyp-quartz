**Why do these white cups have a brown stripe running down them?** Five reviewed cups from the early-May run share one mark: a rust-brown to iron-brown granular streak that runs directionally down the wall, standing a little proud of the pale glaze around it — a single weathered run against a clean cream ground. [[2157]] and [[2158]] carry the clearest examples; [[2163]], [[2165]], and [[2166]] each wear their own version, no two falling the same way.

What the streak actually is — iron in the clay, a mineral, a slip, something pressed into the wall — is never asserted here; the pages describe the rust-brown run exactly as it photographs, following the same caution the series keeps for [[rust-veil|rust veils]] and [[sand-grit|sand grit]]. The term is kept distinct from [[dark-smears|dark smears]] (darker grey gritty veins in a pale glaze) and from [[rust-veil|rust-veil]] (a broad crust that caps or washes a body, not a single directional run). Approved in the 2026-07-13 batch-15 ruling with five members.
