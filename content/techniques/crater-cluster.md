**What are those little dark pits scattered across the white?** Two reviewed cups carry a chain of small dark burst-craters — tiny erupted, pitted blisters that march diagonally across an otherwise pale glaze like a scatter of spent matchheads. [[2168]] and [[2171]] are a close pair: on both, the craters cluster and trail rather than spread evenly, each a small dark pock ringed by lighter glaze.

What caused the craters — a bubble in the glaze, a burst inclusion, something that boiled away in the kiln — is never asserted; the pages describe the pitted chain as it photographs. The term is kept distinct from [[erupted-nodule|erupted nodules]] (large bosses, glassy nodules, or shard-like plates erupting through the wall) and from the star-like *speckle-cluster* look still waiting on its members. Approved in the 2026-07-13 batch-15 ruling with two members.
