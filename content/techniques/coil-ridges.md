**What are the horizontal bands on these pots?** They're the coils themselves. A coil-built pot is stacked up from ropes of clay, and on some pieces those ropes are left visible instead of being smoothed away — the wall reads as a stack of ridges, one per coil. It's [[handbuilding|handbuilding]] with the process left on the surface.

Two pieces of the [[range-1692-1714|wild-clay stretch]] show it clearly: [[1699]], whose store title calls the look a "Layered Ridge Pattern," and [[1701]], a "Coil Vessel" banded the full height of its wall. Several neighbors ([[1698]], [[1700]], [[1708]]) show softer seams and single ridges — those pages describe what's visible without carrying the tag.
