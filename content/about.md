A Penny For Your Pottery is one long, countable project: **10,000 handmade ceramic pieces, made in sequence, each priced one cent higher than the piece before it.**

## The penny rule

The price of a piece is its number times one cent:

| Piece | Price |
|---|---|
| #1 | $0.01 |
| #100 | $1.00 |
| #2,250 | $22.50 |
| #10,000 | $100.00 |

That's the whole pricing system. There are no sales, no repricing, no exceptions. Because prices only ever step upward one cent at a time, the current price is also a public record of how far the series has come — the price tag is the progress bar.

## The pieces

Every piece is handbuilt and individually numbered on its base. Early pieces started as small pinch pots; the forms, clays, and glazes have kept changing as the series goes — which is part of the point. Each piece is a fixed coordinate in the sequence: whatever it is, it's the only piece with that number, made at that moment in the run.

## Numbers before objects

Not every numbered piece exists yet. On the store, pieces that haven't been made yet are listed as **reservations** — buying one is buying the right to that number, and the piece arrives when the series reaches it. This started because people kept asking to reserve pieces they hadn't seen; the surprise turned out to be part of what they wanted.
